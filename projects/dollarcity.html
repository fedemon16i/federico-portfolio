Problem Statement

The platform wasn't working for the people using it

Dollarama (Canada) and DollarCity (LATAM) share a fraud monitoring SaaS to review transaction-linked security footage. On paper it covered the workflow. In practice, employees were spending an enormous amount of time on routine reviews, missing incidents, and struggling with a system that hadn't been designed around how they actually work.

A fraud monitoring + reporting feature had to be integrated while the platform was already in active development ‚Äî with real users, real constraints, and tight timelines. The challenge was to diagnose, redesign, and validate without slowing delivery.

Research Methodology

Two-track research to understand the real gaps

The existing system had been built around assumptions. Before touching any wireframe, I ran both primary and secondary research to ground every decision in observed behavior.

Primary Research
Contextual Inquiries
  • Sessions with telemarketer users across Canada & LATAM stores
  • Observed real workflows in their native environment
  • Captured mental models of non-technical staff
  • Surfaced friction points invisible in analytics
Secondary Research
Competitive Benchmarking
  • Surveyed comparable retail surveillance SaaS platforms
  • Identified interaction patterns for video request flows
  • Benchmarked download queue UX conventions
  • Validated our approach against industry standards

Problems Found

What research surfaced

01
Search was blind
Users had to know exact transaction IDs or timestamps to find footage. There were no contextual filters for product, POS, or incident type ‚Äî forcing manual cross-referencing with paper logs before even opening the tool.
02
Download state was invisible
Once a video was requested, users had no reliable feedback on whether it was queued, processing, or ready. Many would re-request the same clip multiple times, causing server load and user frustration.
03
One flow, four incompatible mental models
The interface was designed for a power-user analyst. Store supervisors ‚Äî who triggered most video requests ‚Äî were navigating a system built for someone with entirely different goals and technical confidence.
Solutions Selected

What we built ‚Äî and why

Contextual POS Search
Filter by store, POS terminal, date range, and product category. Reduces time-to-clip from minutes to seconds for daily analysts.
4-State Download System
Clear visual states: Empty ‚Üí Selected ‚Üí Downloading ‚Üí Complete. Eliminates re-request behavior and gives users confidence the system is working.
Guided Request Form
Step-by-step video request flow for store supervisors. Removes ambiguity and guides non-technical users without overwhelming the analyst view.
Queue Dashboard
Centralized "My Video Requests" view with status tracking. Regional coordinators can oversee cross-store queues at a glance.
Approach

Three questions that drove everything

01 ‚Äî Diagnose the Friction

Find and solve why it was taking an enormous amount of time

Contextual inquiries with telemarketer users across both brands revealed the bottlenecks before touching any interface.

02 ‚Äî Know the Users

Map mental models of non-technical staff

Identified 3 new user personas that didn't exist in the original brief ‚Äî each with distinct workflows and fraud review patterns.

03 ‚Äî Validate & Ship

Test with real users, iterate fast

Useberry sessions with actual telemarketer users validated workflow changes before development handoff, reducing rework.

Key Learnings

What this project taught me

01
Users don't describe their problems ‚Äî they describe their workarounds. Contextual inquiry, not surveys, is how you find the real friction.
02
Designing for the average user means designing for nobody. The four personas required genuinely different interaction models, not just copy changes.
03
Feedback states aren't a nice-to-have. In async workflows like video download, invisible progress kills trust in the entire product.
04
Shipping mid-flight doesn't have to mean compromising quality ‚Äî it means sequencing decisions carefully and earning trust incrementally.
