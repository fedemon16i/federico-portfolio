User Research

What users were struggling with

  1. 1
    Onboarding friction

    Users found integrating the credit card into the wallet complex and time-consuming, leading to abandonment during setup.

  2. 2
    Usability & accessibility gaps

    The UI lacked clarity and consistency, making navigation difficult and excluding users with disabilities.

  3. 3
    Security & privacy concerns

    Users expressed anxiety about their financial data, requiring transparent security communication.

  4. 4
    Financial illiteracy

    Early adopters didn't understand payment dates, minimum payments, or how balances worked.

  5. 5
    No post-launch measurement plan

    Without tracking, identifying new friction points after launch was impossible.

Financial Education Gap

Users get lost in payment options and dates. The repercussions of choosing minimum vs. total payment are not clearly communicated.

Awareness & Navigation Issues

Confusing navigation patterns make paying or getting help feel unnecessarily complex.

Design Strategy

Financial Education That Outlived the Product

One of the core bets in this project was embedding financial education directly into the product — not as a help section, but as contextual nudges throughout every key flow.

We designed in-app explanations for: minimum payment vs. total balance, billing date vs. due date, credit line vs. available balance, and what happens if you only pay the minimum.

The goal was to reduce debt-related abandonment and build user trust by making complex financial concepts scannable and actionable at the moment of decision.

This work continued after acquisition.

Banco Ripley acquired Chek and kept the financial education framework alive under the name Corta y Clara — a dedicated section explaining financial concepts through videos and interactive content. Concepts like Pago Mínimo, Fecha de Facturación, and Estado de Cuenta were built on the groundwork established in this project.

Reflections

Key Learnings

  • Seamless onboarding requires invisible complexity.

    Removing the SERFINSA WebView required backend API work — but the UX win was worth the engineering investment. Legal, tech, and business alignment must come before the design solution.

  • User testing must generate metric-oriented evidence.

    Testing with Useberry allowed us to bring quantitative data to dev and business conversations — not just opinions. This changed how decisions were made post-launch.

  • Build the measurement plan before you ship.

    Defining the event taxonomy and HEARTS framework pre-launch meant we could detect abandonment, success rates, and friction immediately after release — not months later.
